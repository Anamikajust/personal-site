About

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Mathematics at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, where my research focuses on numerical analysis, scientific computing, and the development of efficient algorithms for large-scale mathematical models. I work with Dr. Yangwen Zhang on memory-efficient computational methods for time-fractional partial differential equations using finite element methods and low-rank approximation techniques.

My research aims to reduce the computational cost and memory requirements of large-scale simulations while maintaining mathematical accuracy and reliability. These methods support high-performance scientific computing and have potential applications in areas such as energy systems, environmental modeling, materials science, and data-driven computational technologies.

More broadly, I am interested in designing scalable numerical algorithms for complex differential equations, error analysis, scientific machine learning, and next-generation computational methods that bridge applied mathematics with modern scientific and engineering applications.

I received my M.S. in Mathematics from the University of Louisiana at Lafayette and my B.S. and M.S. in Mathematics from Jashore University of Science and Technology, Bangladesh.

Research

Time-fractional partial differential equations model systems with memory: the solution at every past time step influences the present. Discretizing such problems with L1-type finite element schemes requires storing that entire history, so the memory footprint grows linearly in the number of time steps, n. The ISVD (incremental SVD) framework compresses this history on the fly; my contribution establishes a sharper bound on the compression error this introduces.

Theorem — Compression Error Bound

Let the ISVD compression be applied with truncation tolerance tol over n time steps. Then the accumulated compression error satisfies

‖e‖ = O(√n · tol)

improving on the previously established bound of O(n · tol), via a discrete fractional Grönwall inequality applied to the unsquared error norm.

Prior bound: O(n · tol) — grows linearly with n This work: O(√n · tol) — grows with √n
01 / Theory

Sharper ISVD compression bounds

Improved the incremental-SVD compression error bound for the L1-FEM history matrix from linear to square-root growth in the number of time steps, using a discrete fractional Grönwall inequality applied to the unsquared error norm — the step that preserves the √n improvement.

02 / Method

ISVD-compressed L1-FEM

Extends and adapts Dr. Zhang's ISVD-compressed L1 finite element framework for time-fractional sub-diffusion equations, which compresses the solution history on the fly instead of storing it in full.
